Perfect Music

The scent on its shiny surface,
Black and white
(an enchanting, yet tranquil dress).
Through the window,
its polished lid reflects
restful shades that sieve
(like a breeze sifting moonlight)
a serene mosaic. Calm.
 
It is drizzling.
Softly.
Like a quiet friend leaving you alone.
And yet- not.
Piano.
 
Not quite winter.
Still, autumn has retired.
New rain.
Syrupy, soft.
Music.
 
Sprinkling through the elm
and the oak,
the hornbeam and the London plane,
Nestling snugly in the crisp, scented leaves
And then leaping softly
Pattering drops on Turtle pond
And the old castle
 
This, I don’t see through the window
Only in my mind’s.
Part of my world.
Like the warm comfort of a soft blanket on a November morning
 
A green, gentleness across Fifth Avenue.
It wraps me.
 
The fall is shut
(like the mouth of a sleeping black swan).
But drops of music pitter-patter on my mind.
Not quite jazz.
Not Reverie.
La prélude belle petite?
Non
But still perfect.
 
Wanting nothing more
Just perfect
