Liberation from ToDo Lists

From commandment to reminder

Do ToDo lists intimidate you? They do me.

On the one hand, ToDo lists are incredibly useful. They make sure we get things done, and don’t forget.

On the other hand, they can be intimidating. They are a voice in ones head that continuously mutter, “You have to do me, you have to do me, you have to do me”; ad nauseam. They can be a sort of debt, which one is eternally guilty of not paying. They can even feel like some impending doom…

Hence, do the pros of ToDo lists, compensate for putting up with the cons?

Let’s take a few steps back.

At any given time, we can do only one thing; at most. People who claim to “multi-task”, actually “single-task”; they do not see when one task ends and the other tasks. We decide on what this “one thing” is, based on our circumstances; sometimes consciously, sometimes unconsciously. For example, if we are hungry, we might decide to “do eat”. Often, some aspects of circumstances compete with other factors. For example, if we are on a diet, or no food is available, we don’t eat; even if we are hungry.

When we create ToDo lists, we are merely adding to those circumstances that influence our decisions. In other words, ToDo lists are, not commandments or obligations, but merely reminders. Not doing a ToDo is neither a sin nor a debt.

This, for me at least, is where the liberation hides.

By Nuwan I. Senaratna on April 25, 2021.
